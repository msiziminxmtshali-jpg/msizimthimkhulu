About me

My artistic awakening began not in a classroom, but in the hallowed, resonant space of my local church. At the age of twelve, my world first expanded beyond mere sound into the realm of music, my fingers finding their first language on the cool, ivory keys of the piano. The hymns were my foundation, teaching me structure and melody, a grammar for the emotions I was too young to name.

But soon, a new rhythm called to me—a primal, magnetic pulse that felt like the very heartbeat of the music itself. I fell in love with the drums. For over five years, I dedicated myself to their power, where the discipline of my hands and feet translated thought into thunder and silence. The piano had taught me to speak the language of music; the drums taught me its pulse and its passion.

In the quiet spaces between the beats, another voice began to murmur—a quieter, more introspective one. I started to capture my feelings not with sticks and skins, but with pen and paper, weaving my inner world into rhythmic lines of verse. This was my introduction to the delicate art of songwriting, a craft that soon danced hand-in-hand with a love for storytelling. My private hours became a sanctuary for creation, where the highs and lows of my life were translated into lyrics set to the hopeful key of C major, and fleshed out into the characters and conflicts of short novels like *IT WAS POINTING AT HIM* and *I Once Was In Love*.

These writings, both musical and literary, are more than just projects; they are the maps of an endless journey through the landscapes of my own mind, each piece a captured moment from an ongoing exploration of the heart.
